Livestock Ranching

Extensive farming of animals on large land areas

About Livestock Ranching

Livestock ranching is an extensive agricultural system focused on raising grazing animals such as cattle, sheep, goats, or horses on large areas of land called ranches.

Key Characteristics:

  • Large land requirements
  • Low input costs per unit area
  • Grazing-based feeding systems
  • Primary products: meat, wool, hides
  • Common in arid and semi-arid regions
  • Extensive rather than intensive farming

Common Livestock:

  • Cattle (beef production)
  • Sheep (wool and meat)
  • Goats (milk and meat)
  • Horses (working animals)
